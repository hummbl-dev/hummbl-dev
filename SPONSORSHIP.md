# Sponsorship Policy v0.1 (Draft)

Status: **inactive draft**. HUMMBL is not accepting sponsorship through this
policy. Tier text, account settings, funding links, and announcements remain
held until every launch gate in [the readiness plan](docs/sponsorship-readiness.md)
passes.

## Proposition and identity

The proposed v0.1 identity is Reuben Bowlby's personal `hummbl-dev` account,
with `hummbl-governance` as the provisional flagship. The `mcp-server`
governance package is a first-party integration and discovery path, not
independent adoption evidence.

> Sponsor Reuben's independent maintenance of open-source governance
> primitives that help agentic engineering teams constrain authority, contain
> failures, and produce reviewable evidence.

The actual payout and tax recipient must be verified privately before
activation. Public copy must not imply that HUMMBL, LLC receives sponsorship
income unless that is confirmed.

## Independence

Sponsorship supports maintenance; it does not buy:

- support, consulting, private access, response times, or service levels;
- guaranteed upstream acceptance, merge, release, continued maintenance, or
  roadmap placement;
- implementation, review, or release priority;
- favorable findings, certification, endorsement, or suppression of findings;
- a pre-publication veto, agenda control, or control over technical decisions.

Sponsored and unsponsored changes use the same public acceptance criteria.
A material sponsor relationship affecting a technical decision must be
disclosed, and the decision must receive non-author review. Separate commercial
work must be explicitly scoped and cannot guarantee any outcome listed above.
Security disclosure follows the project's normal policy regardless of payment.
The maintainer may decline or return funding that creates a material conflict.

## Privacy and public reporting

The default is private unless a sponsor separately opts into acknowledgement.
Name, handle, logo, link, and tier acknowledgement are separate permissions.

Public receipts must:

- never publish exact sponsor counts, exact recurring revenue, tier counts, or
  period-over-period deltas at low volume;
- report `below publication threshold` when fewer than five active sponsors
  contribute to a cell, and suppress the related revenue cell;
- use fixed count bands (`5-9`, `10-24`, `25-49`, `50+`) and fixed MRR bands
  (`<$500`, `$500-$999`, `$1,000-$2,499`, `$2,500+`) only after the five-sponsor
  minimum is met;
- omit tier-level reporting unless at least five sponsors are represented in
  that tier, and avoid tier-by-tier public rosters;
- review each release against prior receipts to prevent temporal differencing.

Five is a minimum suppression rule, not proof that publication is safe. Broaden
or omit a band whenever combined public information could identify a sponsor.
Transaction date, amount, contact, location, tax, banking, and payment data stay
private. No sponsor contact database is created for v0.1.

## Legal and marks

Sponsorship tax-deductibility depends on the sponsor's jurisdiction, the
recipient's entity status, and any benefits received; sponsors should consult
their own tax adviser rather than relying on recipient confirmation. Public
name or logo placement is labeled paid sponsorship, not endorsement. A sponsor
grants only limited, revocable permission to display sponsor-supplied marks
and may request removal from future acknowledgements. GitHub and its payment
provider govern payment processing, cancellation, refunds, and transactions
under their applicable terms.

Maintenance receipts report aggregate activity categories. They do not claim
that a sponsor's payment funded a particular output.
