# Thread 03: Insurance Moved First

---

**1/** (122 chars)

**2/** (274 chars)
Late 2025, Berkley Insurance Group added absolute exclusions to Directors & Officers and Errors & Omissions policies. Claims "based upon, arising out of, or attributable to" AI use, deployment, development, or inadequate AI governance -- excluded. No coverage.

**3/** (269 chars)
Hamilton Insurance Group filed similar broad exclusions. The market is bifurcating: firms with documented AI governance artifacts get affirmative coverage. Firms without face absolute exclusions at renewal. This is happening now, not in 2027.

**4/** (260 chars)
Why insurance matters more than regulation: the EU AI Act takes effect Aug 2 2026. Colorado's AI Act is delayed to June 2026. Virginia's AI bill was vetoed. But your D&O renewal is this quarter. Insurance moves faster than legislatures.

**5/** (275 chars)
Carriers now ask on cyber insurance applications: What AI tools are deployed? What governance framework exists? What human-in-the-loop validation? What regulatory compliance posture? If you can't answer these, your premium reflects it -- or you don't get coverage.

**6/** (251 chars)
The phrase to internalize: "uninsurable before unprosecutable." No regulator has fined a US company for AI code failures yet. But insurers are already pricing the risk. When the lawsuit lands, your coverage may already have an AI-shaped hole.

**7/** (273 chars)
The liability chain is closing fast. Moffatt v. Air Canada (2024): deploying company owns chatbot liability. Mobley v. Workday (2025): vendor-as-agent theory certified as class action. The deployer is the default defendant and the insurers know it.

**8/** (268 chars)
Shareholder Caremark claims are the board-level risk. Brewer v. Turner (Del. Ch. 2025) refused to dismiss an oversight claim. Directors deploying AI-assisted code without documented reporting systems face foreseeable Marchand/Caremark exposure.

**9/** (267 chars)
What underwriters want to see: a documented AI governance framework, model inventories, delegation controls, audit trails, incident response SLAs, and evidence of human oversight. Not a policy PDF. Runtime evidence that governance is enforced.

**10/** (270 chars)
This is what governance primitives provide: signed delegation tokens (provenance), append-only audit logs (immutable record), circuit breakers (automatic degradation), kill switches (human override). Evidence generated at runtime, not after the breach.

**11/** (240 chars)
The window between "my insurer doesn't ask about AI" and "my insurer excludes AI claims" is closing. For many companies, it already closed at last renewal. Check your D&O and E&O endorsements. The exclusion may already be there.
