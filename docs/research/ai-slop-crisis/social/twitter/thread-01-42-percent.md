# Thread 01: 42% of Code Is AI-Generated

---

**1/** (78 chars)
42% of committed code is now AI-generated. Here's what happens next. [thread]

**2/** (272 chars)
Sonar surveyed 1,100+ professional devs (Jan 2026). The finding: 42% of the code they commit is AI-generated or AI-assisted. 72% who tried AI coding tools now use them daily. This isn't a pilot anymore. It's the production baseline.

**3/** (275 chars)
Veracode tested 100+ LLMs across 4 languages. AI-generated code contains 2.74x more vulnerabilities than human-written code. 45% failure rate on secure coding benchmarks. The velocity is real. The security gap is also real.

**4/** (269 chars)
Veracode's Spring 2026 update: despite models getting better at syntax (95%+ correctness), security pass rates remain stuck at ~55%. Virtually identical to two years ago. Better autocomplete does not mean better security.

**5/** (270 chars)
The perception gap is the real killer. Stanford (Perry et al., CCS '23, N=47): devs with AI assistants wrote significantly less secure code AND were more likely to believe they wrote secure code. Confidence goes up. Quality goes down.

**6/** (279 chars)
Now add insurance. Berkley Insurance Group wrote absolute AI exclusions into D&O and E&O policies (late 2025). Claims "arising out of" AI use, deployment, or inadequate AI governance are excluded. No coverage. Period.

**7/** (276 chars)
The liability chain: Moffatt v. Air Canada (2024) -- deploying company eats chatbot liability. Mobley v. Workday (2025) -- vendor-as-agent theory certified as class action. The question is not IF companies get sued for AI code failures. It's when.

**8/** (268 chars)
Meanwhile 81% of organizations lack visibility into how AI is actually used in their codebases. You can't govern what you can't see. And your insurer just told you they won't cover what you can't prove you governed.

**9/** (275 chars)
What governance actually looks like: signed delegation tokens (who authorized what), append-only audit logs (immutable record), circuit breakers (automatic degradation), kill switches (human override). Generated at runtime, not reconstructed after the breach.

**10/** (258 chars)
42% AI-generated code + 2.74x vuln ratio + insurance exclusions + liability precedent = a compliance gap that will cost someone nine figures within 24 months. The question is whether you have the governance artifacts before or after discovery.
