# US AI Regulatory Landscape (2024-2026)

**Prepared for:** HUMMBL (Georgia LLC, AI governance infrastructure)
**Date:** 2026-04-05
**Lane:** US federal + state + sector-specific AI regulation
**Companion brief:** EU AI Act coverage (separate document)

---

## Executive Context

The US has no omnibus AI statute. Instead, it operates through (a) sector regulators applying existing authorities (FTC, SEC, EEOC, FDA, OCR, FINRA) to AI, (b) federal procurement rules that shape the vendor market, (c) a fragmented state patchwork led by Colorado, and (d) an active federal preemption push under the Trump administration as of December 2025. The landscape inverted in Jan 2025: the Biden whole-of-government approach was rescinded and replaced with an innovation-forward posture. Most comprehensive state AI bills died or were vetoed in 2025. Colorado's AI Act survived but was delayed to **June 30, 2026**.

## 1. Federal US Posture

### NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0 + GenAI Profile)

- **AI RMF 1.0** released Jan 26, 2023. Voluntary. Four functions: GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE.
- **Generative AI Profile (NIST AI 600-1)** released **July 26, 2024**. Adds 200+ suggested actions across twelve GenAI-specific risk areas (confabulation, IP, CBRN, harmful content, data privacy, value chain opacity, etc.).
- **Who adopts it:** Federal agencies under prior OMB M-24-10 used RMF as baseline; many private enterprises treat it as de facto standard because cyber insurers and procurement officers cite it. ISO/IEC 42001 crosswalks to RMF.
- **Why it matters for HUMMBL:** RMF is the only widely-recognized US governance framework, so "NIST-mapped" is the operative sales language. Under Trump 2.0, RMF has NOT been rescinded; it remains the most likely basis for any federal AI standard under EO 14179.
- **Evidence:** NIST AI 600-1 (https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/ai/NIST.AI.600-1.pdf); NIST AI RMF page (https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework).

### Biden EO 14110 — RESCINDED

- Signed Oct 30, 2023. Required dual-use foundation model reporting under Defense Production Act, NIST red-teaming standards, agency chief AI officers, etc.
- **Rescinded Jan 20, 2025** by Trump's "Initial Rescissions" EO on Day 1.
- What it still governs: nothing, directly. However, derivative artifacts (NIST GenAI Profile, NIST AI Safety Institute work, OMB memos) were built under 14110 and some have survived under new framing.
- **Evidence:** Wikipedia EO 14110; Federal Register EO 14179 (https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/01/31/2025-02172/removing-barriers-to-american-leadership-in-artificial-intelligence).

### Trump EO 14179 — "Removing Barriers to American Leadership in AI"

- Signed **Jan 23, 2025**. Replaces 14110's posture.
- Directs a 180-day AI Action Plan, emphasis on "free from ideological bias," economic competitiveness, national security.
- **Evidence:** White House press release (https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/removing-barriers-to-american-leadership-in-artificial-intelligence/); American Presidency Project (https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/executive-order-14179-removing-barriers-american-leadership-artificial-intelligence).

### Trump EO (Dec 11, 2025) — "Ensuring a National Policy Framework for AI" [state preemption]

- Directs DOJ to create an **AI Litigation Task Force** within 30 days to challenge state AI laws.
- Commerce Department to publish evaluation of "onerous" state AI provisions within 90 days.
- FCC proceeding on federal AI reporting/disclosure standard designed to preempt state law.
- FTC policy statement on unfair/deceptive AI acts.
- **Carve-outs:** child safety, AI compute/data center infrastructure, state government procurement.
- **HUMMBL relevance:** Colorado, NYC 144, California SB 942/AB 2013 are all candidates for DOJ challenge in 2026.
- **Evidence:** WilmerHale (https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/client-alerts/20251212-white-house-issues-one-rule-executive-order-to-curb-state-ai-regulation); Sidley (https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2025/12/unpacking-the-december-11-2025-executive-order); WH EO (https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/12/eliminating-state-law-obstruction-of-national-artificial-intelligence-policy/).

### SEC AI rulemaking — WITHDRAWN

- **Predictive Data Analytics (PDA) proposal** (proposed June 2023 under Gensler): conflicts of interest for broker-dealers/IAs. **Withdrawn June 12, 2025.**
- Current SEC posture: enforcement via existing antifraud provisions ("AI washing" cases against investment advisers who lied about AI capabilities).
- **Aug 2025:** SEC launched internal AI task force.
- **Dec 4, 2025:** Investor Advisory Committee recommended AI-impact disclosure guidance for issuers — not yet adopted.
- **Evidence:** SEC withdrawal notice (https://www.sec.gov/rules-regulations/2025/06/s7-12-23); D&O Diary (https://www.dandodiary.com/2025/12/articles/securities-laws/sec-investor-advisory-committee-recommends-ai-related-disclosure-guidelines/).

### FTC AI enforcement — active

- **Rite Aid (Jan 2024):** five-year facial-recognition ban; FTC alleged unreasonable false-positive rate disproportionately harming Black/Latino customers. First major FTC AI enforcement.
- **Operation AI Comply (Sep 25, 2024):** five-case sweep. Targets included:
  - **DoNotPay** — "world's first robot lawyer" claims; $193,000 settlement.
  - **Automators, Ascend Ecom, Ecommerce Empire Builders** — AI business-opportunity schemes.
  - **Rytr** — AI review generator.
- FTC theory: AI-washing and deceptive claims under Section 5.
- **Evidence:** FTC press release (https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2024/09/ftc-announces-crackdown-deceptive-ai-claims-schemes); Mintz (https://www.mintz.com/insights-center/viewpoints/54731/2024-10-03-ftc-launches-operation-ai-comply-five-enforcement).

### Federal procurement — OMB M-25-21 / M-25-22

- **April 3, 2025:** OMB issued M-25-21 (federal AI use) and M-25-22 (federal AI acquisition), **rescinding** M-24-10 and M-24-18 (Biden-era).
- New posture: "accelerating" AI use, "driving efficient acquisition." Still preserves agency Chief AI Officer role and high-impact AI inventories.
- **GSA:** directed to publish AI procurement guides, standard contract clause language, and an interagency AI acquisition repository.
- **HUMMBL relevance:** federal AI vendors still face governance-artifact expectations, but the compliance bar is more flexible than under M-24-10.
- **Evidence:** M-25-22 PDF (https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/M-25-22-Driving-Efficient-Acquisition-of-Artificial-Intelligence-in-Government.pdf); Wiley alert (https://www.wiley.law/alert-Trump-Administration-Revamps-Guidance-on-Federal-Use-and-Procurement-of-AI).

## 2. State AI Laws (enacted / enforceable)

### Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205) — DELAYED

- Signed May 2024. Originally effective Feb 1, 2026.
- **Aug 28, 2025:** Gov. Polis signed SB 25B-004 delaying effective date to **June 30, 2026**.
- Covers "high-risk AI systems" making consequential decisions (employment, education, housing, financial services, healthcare, legal, government). Duty of reasonable care; risk management program; impact assessments; consumer notice; AG filings.
- **Penalty:** up to **$20,000 per violation** (per affected consumer/transaction) under Colorado Consumer Protection Act. AG exclusive enforcement; 60-day cure period; no private right of action.
- **Evidence:** Colorado General Assembly (https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/sb24-205); Akin (https://www.akingump.com/en/insights/ai-law-and-regulation-tracker/colorado-postpones-implementation-of-colorado-ai-act-sb-24-205); Clark Hill (https://www.clarkhill.com/news-events/news/colorados-ai-law-delayed-until-june-2026-what-the-latest-setback-means-for-businesses/).

### NYC Local Law 144 (AEDT)

- Effective July 5, 2023. Requires independent annual bias audit of automated employment decision tools, public posting of audit summary, candidate notice ≥10 business days pre-use.
- **Penalties:** $500–$1,500 per day, per violation; notice and audit are separate violations.
- **Dec 2025 NY State Comptroller audit:** DCWP enforcement is broken — 75% of 311 calls misrouted; DCWP found 1 violation among 32 companies vs. auditors' 17 potential violations in same set. DLA Piper predicts stricter enforcement phase following.
- **Evidence:** NYS Comptroller (https://www.osc.ny.gov/state-agencies/audits/2025/12/02/enforcement-local-law-144-automated-employment-decision-tools); NYC Rules (https://rules.cityofnewyork.us/rule/automated-employment-decision-tools-2/).

### California

- **SB 1047 (frontier model safety):** **VETOED** by Newsom Sep 29, 2024. Veto not overridden (deadline Nov 30, 2024 passed).
- **SB 942 (California AI Transparency Act):** signed 2024. Required large GenAI providers to offer watermarking + AI-detection tools. **Operative date delayed** from Jan 1, 2026 to **Aug 2, 2026** by AB 853.
- **AB 2013 (training data transparency):** effective **Jan 1, 2026**. Developers of GenAI systems made available to Californians must publicly post summary of training datasets.
- **Evidence:** Morgan Lewis (https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2024/10/california-governor-vetoes-ai-safety-bill-sb-1047-signs-ab-2013-requiring-generative-ai-transparency); TrustArc (https://trustarc.com/resource/california-ai-transparency-laws-sb942-ab2013/).

### Texas — TRAIGA (HB 149)

- Signed **June 22, 2025**. Effective **Jan 1, 2026**. Third comprehensive state AI law.
- Pared back from original EU-style draft. Prohibits: AI systems designed to manipulate causing harm; discrimination on protected characteristics; CSAM generation; government social scoring.
- Creates AI Advisory Council + 36-month regulatory sandbox.
- **Evidence:** K&L Gates (https://www.klgates.com/Pared-Back-Version-of-the-Texas-Responsible-Artificial-Intelligence-Governance-Act-Signed-Into-Law-6-24-2025); DLA Piper (https://www.dlapiper.com/en/insights/publications/2025/06/texas-adopts-the-responsible-ai-governance-act).

### Utah AI Policy Act (SB 149)

- Effective May 1, 2024. Requires disclosure when consumers interact with GenAI in regulated occupations and high-risk contexts; creates Office of AI Policy + learning lab. Innovation-oriented, not prescriptive. **Not verified** for latest amendment status.

### Tennessee ELVIS Act

- Signed 2024. Targets AI voice/likeness cloning (music industry focus). Not a general AI law. **Not verified** on exact effective date.

### Virginia HB 2094 — VETOED

- Passed legislature Feb 20, 2025. **Vetoed by Gov. Youngkin March 24, 2025.** Cited $30M estimated compliance cost, harm to startups. Would have been second comprehensive state AI law after Colorado.
- **Evidence:** Ogletree (https://ogletree.com/insights-resources/blog-posts/virginia-governor-vetoes-artificial-intelligence-bill-hb-2094-what-the-veto-means-for-businesses/); IAPP (https://iapp.org/news/a/virginia-governor-vetoes-ai-bill).

### Connecticut SB 2

- Governor Lamont (D) threatened veto in 2024; bill stalled. Sen. Maroney reintroduced for 2025 session. **Not verified** as signed.

### Consumer privacy acts with AI provisions

- **Virginia VCDPA, Connecticut CTDPA, Colorado CPA, California CPRA, Texas TDPSA, Oregon OCPA, Delaware DPDPA** all contain profiling/automated-decision-making opt-out rights. These function as de facto AI regulation for covered businesses. **Not verified** on full 2026 enforcement posture across all states.

## 3. Sector-Specific Rules

### HIPAA + AI / ACA Section 1557

- **HHS OCR final rule (May 6, 2024)** under ACA Section 1557: covered entities must not discriminate via "patient care decision support tools" (clinical algorithms, predictive analytics, AI). Applicability date **March 2, 2025** (300 days post-effective).
- Covered entity owns the discrimination risk — not the tool vendor. Must make "reasonable efforts to mitigate."
- **OCR Jan 2025 Dear Colleague letter** reinforced enforcement posture.
- **Evidence:** Federal Register (https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/05/06/2024-08711/nondiscrimination-in-health-programs-and-activities); Bipartisan Policy Center (https://bipartisanpolicy.org/article/navigating-ai-in-health-care-hhss-nondiscrimination-final-rule-is-in-effect/).

### FINRA / SEC (financial services)

- **FINRA Regulatory Notice 24-09 (June 27, 2024):** existing Rules 3110 (supervision) and 2210 (communications) apply to GenAI. No new rules, but reinforces model risk management, data integrity, human review expectations.
- FINRA 2026 Annual Oversight Report continues GenAI focus.
- **Evidence:** FINRA 24-09 (https://www.finra.org/rules-guidance/notices/24-09); Mayer Brown (https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2024/07/finra-reminds-members-of-regulatory-obligations-when-using-generative-artificial-intelligence-ai-and-large-language-models).

### FDA AI/ML medical devices

- **Final guidance (Dec 2024):** Marketing Submission Recommendations for a **Predetermined Change Control Plan (PCCP)** for AI-Enabled Device Software Functions. Lets manufacturers pre-approve a modification roadmap without resubmission for each update.
- **Draft guidance (Jan 6, 2025):** AI-Enabled Device Software Functions: Lifecycle Management and Marketing Submission Recommendations.
- Core elements: description of modifications, modification protocol, impact assessment.
- **Evidence:** FDA PCCP page (https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/marketing-submission-recommendations-predetermined-change-control-plan-artificial-intelligence); Ropes & Gray (https://www.ropesgray.com/en/insights/alerts/2024/12/fda-finalizes-guidance-on-predetermined-change-control-plans-for-ai-enabled-device).

### EEOC on AI hiring tools

- **EEOC ADA guidance (May 2022)** + **Title VII guidance (May 2023):** existing anti-discrimination law applies to AI/algorithmic selection. Employer liable for vendor tool outcomes.
- **iTutorGroup consent decree (Aug 2023, approved Sep 2023):** first EEOC AI enforcement. Automated hiring tool rejected female applicants ≥55 and male ≥60; **$365,000** settlement, 200+ applicants affected.
- **Mobley v. Workday (ND Cal, ongoing):** EEOC amicus argues AI **vendors** can be "agents" of employers under Title VII/ADA/ADEA — novel liability theory extending beyond the employer.
- **Evidence:** Sullivan & Cromwell (https://www.sullcrom.com/insights/blogs/2023/August/EEOC-Settles-First-AI-Discrimination-Lawsuit); Seyfarth Workday analysis (https://www.seyfarth.com/news-insights/legal-update-eeoc-argues-vendors-using-artificial-intelligence-tools-are-subject-to-title-vii-the-ada-and-adea-under-novel-theories-in-workday-litigation.html).

## 4. Insurance & Liability

### Cyber insurance — AI underwriting questions

Carriers now ask (per HUB International, Insurance Business America):
- What AI tools are deployed and how
- Training data provenance and sensitivity
- Documented AI governance framework (policies, model inventories)
- Human-in-the-loop / validation processes
- Regulatory compliance posture (NIST RMF, state laws)
- First- and third-party AI liability exposure

### D&O and E&O — AI exclusions creeping in

- **Berkley Insurance Group** introduced **absolute AI exclusion** for D&O/E&O/Fiduciary policies (late 2025): excludes claims "based upon, arising out of, or attributable to" AI use, deployment, development, AI-generated content, failure to detect AI content, inadequate AI governance, chatbot communications.
- **Hamilton Insurance Group** filed similar broad exclusions late 2025.
- **Cyber policies:** AI exclusions less common here (as of late 2025) — carriers still treating AI-driven incidents as cyber events.
- **Market bifurcation forming:** firms with documented AI governance artifacts get affirmative coverage; firms without face absolute exclusions at renewal.
- **Evidence:** Lexology carrier exclusion analysis (https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=b76e0dba-d9a8-44f1-9f5d-6fbd0a22f6b6); HUB International (https://www.hubinternational.com/products/proex/the-advocate/2025/12/ai-risk-and-insurance/); Harvard Corp Gov (https://corpgov.law.harvard.edu/2025/09/22/the-hidden-c-suite-risk-of-ai-failures/).

### Known AI liability cases

- **Moffatt v. Air Canada, 2024 BCCRT 149 (Feb 14, 2024):** BC Civil Resolution Tribunal held Air Canada liable CAN$812.02 for negligent misrepresentation by its chatbot. Rejected argument that chatbot is separate entity; company owns all website content. Widely cited globally as first consumer-chatbot liability precedent.
- **Mobley v. Workday (ND Cal):** pending — vendor liability theory.
- **Multiple FTC AI-washing cases:** see Section 1.
- **Copyright suits (NYT v. OpenAI, Authors Guild, Getty v. Stability):** ongoing; outside this brief's scope.
- **Evidence:** ABA Business Law Today (https://www.americanbar.org/groups/business_law/resources/business-law-today/2024-february/bc-tribunal-confirms-companies-remain-liable-information-provided-ai-chatbot/).
