## Category Synthesis

### Dominant IA Pattern
**Universal top-nav shape:** `Platform/Product | Solutions | Resources/Learn | Pricing | Company | Login/Demo`. 6 of 7 follow this. Credo AI and Fiddler add `Solutions` as a vertical-cut; only Arize elevates OSS (Phoenix) into the primary nav as a funnel wedge. **Pricing lives in nav in 5/7** — the governance-pure plays (Credo, Apiiro) hide it.

### Dominant Trust-Signal Pattern
Logo walls dominate (7/7). **Two schools:**
1. **Enterprise prestige** (Apiiro, Fiddler, Credo): financial-services logos (BlackRock, Mastercard, DTCC, U.S. Navy) — sell on buyer peer-matching.
2. **Dev adoption numbers** (Qodo, Arize, Aikido, Snyk): scale metrics (848K installs, 1T spans, 50k orgs, 288% ROI) — sell on bottom-up proof.

HUMMBL has neither yet. The Aikido pattern (org count + dev count + star rating + time-to-value) is the most attainable synthetic.

### Pricing Posture
- **Public pricing:** Qodo, Arize, Aikido, Snyk (4/7) — all have self-serve funnels
- **Contact-sales only:** Apiiro, Credo AI, Fiddler (3/7) — all enterprise/governance-first
- **Correlation:** Self-serve product = public pricing. Governance-consulting hybrid = hidden pricing.

### Positioning Axes
Three clusters emerge:
- **Developer-first AppSec:** Snyk, Aikido, Qodo — speed + free tier + dev language
- **Enterprise AppSec / Code-risk:** Apiiro — regulated-vertical logos, no metrics, demo-only
- **AI observability / governance:** Arize (dev-first), Fiddler (enterprise), Credo AI (compliance-first)

Axes: **(a)** dev-first ↔ compliance-first, **(b)** observability ↔ governance ↔ security, **(c)** self-serve ↔ contact-sales.

### Where HUMMBL Can Stand Out
1. **Own the "agent governance infrastructure" category term** — nobody uses "infrastructure" framing. Credo says "platform," Fiddler says "control plane." "Infrastructure" implies plumbing/primitives, which matches HUMMBL's contract/ledger/bus architecture.
2. **Publish pricing + self-serve the governance category** — Credo, Apiiro, Fiddler all hide pricing. A transparent free tier in governance would be unique.
3. **Stdlib-only + on-prem as trust claims** — none of the 7 mention dependency count or supply-chain hygiene ATF. For a governance buyer, "zero third-party runtime deps" is an IP differentiator.
4. **Contract-schema-as-interface** — use "contracts" as the positioning noun (like Fiddler owns "control plane"). None of the 7 own this.
5. **Developer time-to-value promise** — Aikido's "30sec" is the only one. HUMMBL could claim "first audit log in 60 seconds."
6. **Show outcome metrics ATF** — 5/7 don't. Pick 3 numbers (briefings generated, audit events logged, adapters governed) and lead with them.
7. **Headline brevity** — "Govern AI Everywhere" (Credo, 3 words) and "Ship Agents that Work" (Arize, 4 words) win on recall. Qodo's 5-clause headline is a warning.

### Recommendation
HUMMBL's ideal hero synthesis from the audit: **Credo's headline brevity + Aikido's self-serve + Snyk's ROI metric stack + Arize's OSS wedge + Fiddler's architectural noun ("control plane" / "infrastructure")**. Avoid the Apiiro contact-sales-only model until enterprise logos exist.
