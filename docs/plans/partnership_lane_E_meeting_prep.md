# Partnership Formalization Meeting — Reverse-Prompting Framework
**Lane E Deliverable | Reuben Bowlby + Daniel Matha | 2026-04-05**

---

## 1. Meeting Structure (2-Hour Session)

| # | Section | Time | Purpose |
|---|---------|------|---------|
| 1 | Opening + Emotional Check-In | 10 min | Ground the conversation, name the stakes |
| 2 | Values + Vision Alignment | 15 min | Confirm shared picture before negotiating |
| 3 | Current-State Acknowledgment | 15 min | Name gaps, frictions, unresolved debt |
| 4 | Role Clarity | 15 min | Who does what, who is accountable for what |
| 5 | IP + Equity (highest load) | 30 min | Surface assumptions, propose splits |
| 6 | Comp + Revenue Share | 20 min | Fix underpayment, define ongoing formula |
| 7 | Decision Rights | 10 min | Who decides what, escalation paths |
| 8 | Closing + Commitments | 5 min | Capture decisions, next steps, emotional close |

**Total: 120 minutes.** Build in a 5-min stretch between sections 5 and 6.

---

## 2. Reverse-Prompting Prompt Library

Both principals write answers **in parallel** (2-3 min each) before discussing. No interruptions while writing. Answers are read aloud, then compared.

### 2.1 Opening Alignment Prompts (pick 5-6)

1. "In one sentence: what are we actually building together, and for whom?"
2. "What would have to be true in 3 years for you to say this partnership worked?"
3. "What do you most want this meeting to produce — and what do you most fear it will produce?"
4. "What's the thing you've been holding back saying about our partnership?"
5. "If this conversation goes well, what will you feel walking out of the room?"
6. "What's one thing the OTHER person does that you want to acknowledge publicly?"
7. "On a scale of 1-10, how clear is our partnership structure today? Why that number?"
8. "What's the version of this partnership we'd regret in 5 years?"

### 2.2 Role Clarity Prompts (pick 5-6)

1. "What are the 3 things only YOU can do in this company?"
2. "What are the 3 things you're currently doing that someone else should own?"
3. "Describe the other person's role in one sentence — as you understand it today."
4. "Where do our responsibilities overlap in ways that create friction?"
5. "If we hired a Chief of Staff tomorrow, what would you hand off first?"
6. "What title describes what you ACTUALLY do (not what the org chart says)?"
7. "What do you want the other person to stop doing? Start doing? Keep doing?"
8. "Who is the 'face' of HUMMBL externally — and does that match reality?"

### 2.3 IP + Equity Prompts (pick 6-7)

1. "List every piece of IP you personally brought into this partnership. Don't edit."
2. "List every piece of IP the OTHER person brought in. Be generous."
3. "What IP has been created jointly since we started working together?"
4. "If we split tomorrow, what do you take, what does the other take, what is joint?"
5. "What equity split feels fair to you right now? Say the number. Explain the reasoning."
6. "What split would feel UNFAIR — and why?"
7. "How should equity change if one of us contributes materially more in year 2?"
8. "What's the vesting schedule you'd want if you were the one being vested?"
9. "If we don't agree on equity today, what's our fallback mechanism?"

### 2.4 Comp + Revenue Share Prompts (pick 5-6)

1. "What's the minimum monthly comp you need to stop subsidizing this company personally?"
2. "What did you actually earn from HUMMBL in the last 6 months? Say the number."
3. "What should the comp formula be: fixed salary, revenue share, or hybrid? Why?"
4. "If the company earns $X next month, how should that split between the two of us?"
5. "When do we revisit comp — monthly, quarterly, at revenue milestones?"
6. "What's the comp ratio you'd accept if one principal is clearly carrying more load?"
7. "If we can only pay ONE of us fully next quarter, how do we decide which?"
8. "What's the back-pay question we're both avoiding?"

### 2.5 Decision Rights Prompts (pick 5-6)

1. "What decisions should require BOTH of us? Name 3."
2. "What decisions should EITHER of us be able to make alone? Name 3."
3. "What decisions have we been making without clarity about who owns them?"
4. "When we disagree, what's our tiebreaker? (Third party? Domain ownership? Escalation?)"
5. "Who has final say on: hiring, pricing, product direction, external deals, legal?"
6. "What's a decision you've made recently that you wish we'd made together?"
7. "What's a decision the other person made that you'd want veto rights over?"
8. "If we can't reach consensus, how long do we try before defaulting to the tiebreaker?"

### 2.6 Closing Commitment Prompts (pick 3-4)

1. "What did you DECIDE today that you're willing to put in writing?"
2. "What's still unresolved, and when will we resolve it?"
3. "What's one thing you're committing to do differently starting Monday?"
4. "On a scale of 1-10, how do you feel about this partnership RIGHT NOW vs when we started?"
5. "What do you need from me in the next 30 days to feel this is working?"

## 3. Anti-Pattern Prompts to AVOID

Do NOT use prompts that:

- **Trigger legal posturing**: "What would you sue me for?" / "What's your walkaway number?"
- **Lock positions prematurely**: "State your final equity demand." / "Give me your bottom line."
- **Create defensiveness**: "Why haven't you been contributing more?" / "Why do you think you deserve that?"
- **Assume bad faith**: "What are you hiding?" / "Why didn't you tell me X?"
- **Force binary framing**: "Is it 50/50 or not?" / "Are you in or out?"
- **Weaponize history**: "Remember when you said X in 2024?" (Use "What did we learn from..." instead)
- **Lead the witness**: "Don't you think Dan deserves more because of JSR?" (Let them surface it.)

**Rule of thumb:** If the prompt has a "right answer" in your head before they answer, rewrite it.

## 4. AI-Assistance Protocol

**Claude's role:** facilitator, not arbiter.

**Responsibilities:**
1. **Read prompts aloud** (or display) — one at a time, no skipping ahead
2. **Enforce parallel writing** — both principals write before either speaks
3. **Capture answers verbatim** in a structured table (prompt | Reuben | Daniel | delta)
4. **Flag contradictions** explicitly: *"Reuben said X about role clarity; Daniel said Y. Want to discuss?"*
5. **Propose synthesis** without choosing: *"Here are three ways to reconcile those answers. Which resonates?"*
6. **Hold the clock** — surface when a section is running over
7. **Never take a position** on equity, comp, or IP — only reflect and synthesize
8. **Produce meeting summary** at the end: decisions, open items, action items, emotional temperature

**Explicit non-responsibilities:**
- Does NOT recommend equity splits
- Does NOT judge fairness
- Does NOT resolve disagreements — escalates them back to principals

## 6. Decision Forcing Functions

### MUST decide today (non-negotiable):
1. **Equity split** — even if "provisional for 90 days, revisit"
2. **Monthly comp floor** for each principal (stop underpayment bleeding)
3. **IP ownership rule** for work created going forward
4. **Tiebreaker mechanism** for deadlocked decisions
5. **Umbrella entity direction** — research institute model: yes / no / needs more info

### CAN defer to follow-up (with dates):
- Final operating agreement legal language (→ lawyer, 2 weeks)
- Vesting cliff specifics (→ next meeting, 1 week)
- External investor strategy (→ separate session)
- Title formalization (→ async, 3 days)

### Anti-postponement mechanisms:
- **Timeboxed decisions**: "We decide in the next 10 min or we default to X"
- **Provisional commitments**: "90-day trial of 55/45, revisit with data"
- **Pre-committed defaults**: "If we don't decide equity, it's 50/50 and neither of us loves that"
- **Written record**: Claude captures each decision in real-time, read back before closing
- **Next-meeting date set** before leaving the room

## 7. Emotional Considerations

| Landmine | Why It's Loaded | How to Defuse |
|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Reuben's underpayment | Months of personal subsidy, quiet resentment | Name it directly in opening; treat back-pay as a line item, not a grievance |
| Dan's JSR ownership | Pre-existing IP, could feel like "hidden" asset | Surface early as factual baseline; no judgment, just disclosure |
| Technical vs business power asymmetry | Reuben: "I built it." Dan: "I sell it." Both true. | Validate BOTH contributions explicitly in opening alignment |
| "Co-founder" label without paperwork | Feels like broken promise | Acknowledge the gap, don't relitigate how it happened |
| External-facing vs internal roles | Dan is the face; Reuben might feel invisible | Role clarity section addresses this directly |
| Research institute vs VC tension | Different risk appetites | Frame as a STRATEGY decision, not a values judgment |

**Ground rules for emotional safety:**
- "We're both here because we want this to work." (State at opening.)
- No side-conversations. No phones.
- Either party can call a 5-min break without explaining.
- Disagreement ≠ attack.
- "I feel" statements encouraged; "You always" statements flagged.
