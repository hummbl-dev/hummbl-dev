# HUMMBL HUMINT Foundation v0.1

**Status: CANDIDATE — RESEARCH FIRST — NON-CANON — NO BRAND PROMOTION YET**

Issue: hummbl-dev/hummbl-dev#143

## Mission

Before `HUMMBL HUMINT` is adopted as a public identity, repository, doctrine, or editorial program, ground it in the actual history, terminology, collection practices, evaluation methods, and legal/ethical boundaries of human intelligence.

The existing internal idea pack (`founder-mode/docs/research/idea-packs/2026-05-16-intelligence-lexicon-agent-taxonomy.md`) is a useful starting point but has a narrower purpose: it aligns IC/DoD-inspired evidence-channel metaphors with HUMMBL terminology and explicitly disclaims government authority or collection power. It is not yet a history or taxonomy of HUMINT itself.

## Research questions

### Historical lineage

1. What is the documented history of HUMINT as a discipline?
2. How has the terminology evolved across eras, organizations, and jurisdictions?
3. What are the major schools, doctrines, and frameworks?
4. What failures, scandals, and reforms shaped the discipline?
5. What is the relationship between HUMINT and adjacent disciplines (SIGINT, OSINT, IMINT, MASINT, SOF, diplomacy, journalism, social science)?

### Collection taxonomy

1. What are the standard categories of human intelligence collection?
2. How do different organizations classify sources, methods, and products?
3. What is the distinction between overt, covert, and clandestine collection?
4. What are the ethical and legal frameworks governing each?
5. How do modern technologies (digital, social, biometric) change the taxonomy?

### Evaluation methods

1. How is human-source information evaluated for reliability and credibility?
2. What are the standard evaluation frameworks (e.g., Admiralty Code, NATO system, ICD 203)?
3. How do cognitive biases affect source evaluation?
4. How is deception detected and managed?
5. What is the role of counterintelligence in evaluation?

### Ethical and legal boundaries

1. What are the legal frameworks governing HUMINT in democratic societies?
2. What are the historical ethical failures and their consequences?
3. What are the boundaries between HUMINT and human rights?
4. How do oversight mechanisms work (or fail)?
5. What are the transferable ethical principles for non-governmental intelligence work?

### Transfer to HUMMBL context

1. Which HUMINT concepts are transferable to agent-mediated intelligence?
2. Which concepts are explicitly not transferable (e.g., coercion, deception, covert recruitment)?
3. How does the HUMMBL evidence-channel metaphor map to HUMINT taxonomy?
4. What is the ethical boundary for agent-mediated human-source interaction?
5. What terminology should HUMMBL adopt, adapt, or avoid?

## Source posture

### Primary sources to consult

- Declassified government manuals and doctrine documents
- Academic intelligence studies literature
- Congressional/parliamentary oversight reports
- Historical case studies (published, declassified)
- Professional association standards (e.g., IAFIE)
- International law and human rights frameworks

### Source evaluation

- All sources must be classified by fact posture (primary, secondary, tertiary)
- Government sources must be marked with declassification status
- Academic sources must be marked with peer-review status
- Claims must be separated from interpretations
- Uncertainty must be explicit

### Prohibited sources

- Classified or leaked current operational materials
- Sources that identify current intelligence officers or sources
- Materials obtained through unauthorized access
- Advocacy materials presented as neutral analysis

## Ethical transfer boundaries

### Transferable

- Evaluation frameworks (reliability/credibility scoring)
- Source protection principles (anonymity, consent)
- Counter-deception awareness
- Structured analytic techniques
- Evidence weighting and confidence assessment
- Chain-of-custody and provenance tracking

### Not transferable

- Coercion or pressure tactics
- Deception or manipulation of sources
- Covert recruitment
- Exploitation of vulnerability
- Surveillance without consent or legal authority
- Identity manipulation
- Any activity that would be illegal if performed by a private actor

### HUMMBL-specific constraints

- Agent-mediated human interaction must be transparent (agent discloses it is an agent)
- No agent may recruit, coerce, or deceive a human source
- All human-source information must be consented and attributable
- The HUMMBL evidence-channel metaphor is epistemic, not operational
- HUMMBL HUMINT is a research and terminology program, not a collection program

## Terminology framework

### Adopt

Terms that transfer cleanly with HUMMBL's epistemic (not operational) framing:

- source reliability
- information credibility
- evidence weighting
- structured analytic technique
- chain of custody
- provenance

### Adapt

Terms that need modification for HUMMBL context:

- "collection" → "evidence gathering" (consent-based, transparent)
- "source" → "contributor" or "evidence source" (no recruitment implied)
- "handling" → "stewardship" (no operational control implied)
- "product" → "synthesis" or "assessment" (no classified product implied)

### Avoid

Terms that carry operational or government authority implications:

- "agent" (in the intelligence sense — HUMMBL uses "agent" for AI agents)
- "case officer"
- "recruitment"
- "tasking"
- "operational"
- "clandestine"
- "covert"

## Non-goals

- Does not create a HUMMBL HUMINT repository
- Does not adopt HUMINT as a public brand identity
- Does not define operational collection procedures
- Does not claim government authority or collection power
- Does not train agents in HUMINT techniques
- Does not publish classified or sensitive materials
