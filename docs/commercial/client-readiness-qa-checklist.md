# Client Readiness QA Checklist

Status: public-safe checklist  
Source issues: #75, #76

## Purpose

Use this checklist to review HUMMBL buyer-facing offers, checkout links, and landing pages from a plain-English client-success perspective.

This checklist is suitable for Jenna-ready first-pass review because it requires buyer empathy, attention to detail, and clear communication, not technical architecture.

## Offer QA table

| Offer | Price | Link opens? | Product name clear? | Buyer understands outcome? | Next step clear? | Trust gap | Suggested wording fix |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|---|

## Review prompts

1. What would a first-time buyer think this offer means?
2. What would they expect to receive?
3. What would they need to send HUMMBL?
4. What happens after purchase?
5. What might feel risky or unclear?
6. What sounds too technical, vague, or internally named?
7. What terms need a plain-English explanation?
8. What would stop someone from buying?
9. Which offer should a confused buyer choose first?
10. Which offer feels too high-commitment for direct checkout?

## Trust checks

- Is the buyer told who the offer is for?
- Is the deliverable named clearly?
- Is the required input named clearly?
- Is the timeline or next step visible?
- Are boundaries and exclusions visible?
- Are refund/cancellation/scope questions surfaced?
- Is there a way to ask for invoice, PO, NDA, or custom scope?
- Is there a support/contact path?

## Privacy boundaries

Do not include real customer data, private payment data, private Jenna-specific context, or internal secrets in this checklist.
