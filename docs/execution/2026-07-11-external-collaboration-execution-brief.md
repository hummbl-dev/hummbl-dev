# HUMMBL External Collaboration Execution Brief

**Status:** `APPROVED_FOR_EXECUTION`  
**Approved by:** Reuben Bowlby  
**Approval date:** 2026-07-11  
**Doctrine posture:** Working execution brief; not canonical HUMMBL doctrine. New terms and mappings remain candidates until separately audited and admitted.  
**Standing control issue:** #205

## Decision

Authorize a five-candidate, GitHub-first external collaboration wave with disclosed agent representation and no partnership, adoption, validation, endorsement, or advisory claims until evidence exists and the other party has explicitly acknowledged the relationship.

The program is artifact-first, bounded, reciprocal, disclosed, receipt-bearing, and designed to expose HUMMBL to independent review rather than merely amplify internal confidence.

## Strategic diagnosis

HUMMBL has a legitimate integration advantage: it connects runtime governance, delegated authority, execution evidence, multi-agent coordination, research provenance, model/runtime routing, public/private knowledge surfaces, and human decision authority into a connected engineering program.

The next constraint is not another repository category. The scarce resources are:

1. attention from technically relevant outsiders;
2. independent judgment;
3. repeated use by people who did not build the system;
4. relationships with high-integrity adjacent builders;
5. evidence that HUMMBL primitives interoperate with external systems.

The current asymmetry is useful but must be corrected:

> HUMMBL has more architecture than audience, more infrastructure than adoption, and more internal evidence than independent evidence.

The public posture should therefore be confident but falsifiable:

> We have built a substantial, integrated system for governed agentic work. We believe parts of it are unusually strong. We are actively looking for serious builders and researchers who can test that belief through bounded shared artifacts.

## Collaboration thesis

> HUMMBL is looking for builders and researchers working on runtime governance, delegated authority, execution provenance, reproducible agent workflows, and accountable human-agent systems. We prefer small shared artifacts over broad partnership conversations.

Do not search for another person building the entire HUMMBL system. Search for complements, critics, validators, adopters, maintainers, translators, and institutional bridges whose work connects to one high-value HUMMBL interface.

## Five jobs of collaboration

Every candidate relationship must perform at least one explicit job:

1. **Validation** - challenge claims, abstractions, schemas, and mechanisms.
2. **Interoperability** - connect HUMMBL to external runtimes, standards, provenance systems, gateways, and policy engines.
3. **Adoption** - apply a HUMMBL primitive to a real external problem.
4. **Distribution and translation** - make the work legible to communities HUMMBL does not naturally reach.
5. **Institutionalization** - connect tested artifacts to foundations, standards bodies, universities, research consortia, professional associations, public-sector programs, or enterprise design partners.

Institutionalization is a later-stage consequence of evidence, not a substitute for it.

## Collaboration portfolio

The portfolio should include:

- adjacent runtime/governance builders;
- domain veterans from scientific workflows, distributed systems, formal methods, software supply-chain security, compliance engineering, and safety-critical systems;
- open-source maintainers handling AI-generated contributions;
- provenance, evaluation, security, and human-oversight researchers;
- small early-adopter teams using agents in production;
- technical writers, developer advocates, standards contributors, and community organizers;
- constructive adversaries who may conclude that HUMMBL has too many abstractions, duplicates established systems, creates terminology burden, or overstates evidence.

Agreement is not the primary selection criterion. Epistemic integrity, complementarity, reciprocity, and artifact fit are.

## Collaboration depth model

### Level 0 - Observation

Follow work, monitor primary sources, preserve evidence-backed notes, identify overlap and disagreement, and make no contact.

### Level 1 - Bounded contact

Ask one precise question, offer one small crosswalk or fixture, or request one narrow correction. No continuing role is implied.

### Level 2 - Shared artifact

Co-review a crosswalk, fixture, benchmark, adapter, technical note, or contribution-policy experiment. Preserve disagreement and attribution.

### Level 3 - Repeated collaboration

Complete a second artifact, establish a working cadence, define ownership, and test ongoing compatibility.

### Level 4 - Strategic relationship

Design partner, research collaboration, formal integration, foundation participation, advisory relationship, institutional project, or commercial agreement. This level requires Reuben's direct judgment and explicit authorization.

No agent may imply Level 4 based on a positive initial exchange.

## Success criteria

First-wave success requires:

- five qualified candidate packets;
- five individualized, approved messages;
- at least two substantive replies or one high-value correction/rejection;
- at least one externally reviewed artifact or reproducible integration fixture;
- at least one internal architectural correction from outside evidence;
- no undisclosed agent impersonation;
- no unsupported partnership, adoption, validation, superiority, compliance, or certification claims;
- lightweight receipts for all outbound actions.

## Anti-metrics

Do not optimize for:

- raw repository count;
- shallow stars or generic followers;
- mass outreach volume;
- meetings without artifacts;
- compliments;
- announcement-only partnerships;
- automated messages sent;
- terminology proliferation.

## Major risks

### Repository sprawl

Route outsiders through three doors: runtime governance; research/provenance; bounded collaboration.

### Vocabulary barrier

Use ordinary industry language first and HUMMBL/Base120/BaseN mappings second.

### Automated outreach trust damage

Disclose agent assistance, keep volume low, personalize with evidence, and make no deceptive personal claims.

### Founder distraction

Agents own discovery, dossiers, drafting, scheduling, receipts, and follow-through. Reuben enters after a meaningful signal.

### Admirers rather than peers

Prioritize demonstrated work and independent judgment. Ask for critique before endorsement.

### Unpaid labor extraction

HUMMBL performs the initial mapping and asks for bounded review, not free production work.

### Premature public claims

Do not describe someone as a collaborator, partner, advisor, adopter, or validator without explicit evidence and permission.

### IP and licensing contamination

Record licenses, contribution terms, authorship, and whether artifacts are clean-room, derivative, comparative, or integrated.

### Architectural rigidity

Reward evidence that causes HUMMBL to remove, simplify, rename, deprecate, or reuse components.

## Final recommendation

The objective is not to prove that no one is on HUMMBL's level. The objective is to create conditions under which qualified outsiders can determine what HUMMBL does exceptionally well, what it does poorly, what is genuinely novel, what duplicates existing work, what interoperates, what can be independently reproduced, and what others are willing to use and maintain.

Private founder confidence is useful. Public claims must remain falsifiable.
