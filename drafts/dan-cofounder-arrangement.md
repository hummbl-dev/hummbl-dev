## Part 2: Video Call Script

Print this out or have it open on a second screen. It's structured as a conversation flow, not a monologue.

### Opening (2 min)

> "Hey Dan, thanks for making time for this. I want to say upfront — this isn't a difficult conversation. We're aligned on building something great together. I just want to get the structure right before we launch so neither of us has to worry about it later."

*Why*: Sets the tone as collaborative, not adversarial. Reduces Dan's anxiety about what's coming.

---

### Topic 1: Entity Structure (5 min)

> "Here's how I see the two entities working:
>
> **foundermode-ai** is our shared thing. The app, the platform, Peptide Checker — that's both of us. You take majority since you initiated the projects and put in capital for the hardware and accounts.
>
> **HUMMBL** is my consulting and governance business. Base120, the MCP server, the compliance tooling, hummbl.io — that stays mine. I'd like to offer you a small advisory stake in HUMMBL, maybe 5%, as a sign of partnership. No obligations, just upside if it takes off.
>
> Does that framing make sense to you?"

*Wait for Dan to respond.* He should agree to this easily because he gets majority on his products and a free 5% of yours.

---

### Topic 2: Equity Split (8 min)

> "For foundermode-ai, I'm thinking 55/45 or 50/50, with you as majority. You brought the hardware investment, the Apple and GCP accounts, and the app product direction. I built the platform — the backend, CI, testing, governance, the briefing system, the dashboard. The founder-mode repo actually started in my GitHub org before I transferred it over to foundermode-ai.
>
> I think near-equal reflects the reality that we're both essential. What feels right to you — 55/45 or 50/50?"

*Let Dan respond.* Three scenarios:

**If Dan says 50/50 or 55/45**: Great. Agree and move on.

**If Dan says 60/40**:
> "I can work with that, but let me share my thinking. I originated the founder-mode concept, built the SENSE framework, wrote 7,700 tests, 103 service modules, and 14 CI workflows. The repo started in my org. At 60/40, that's a significant discount on the engineering contribution. I think 55/45 is fairer. Can we meet there?"

**If Dan pushes below 60/40**:
> "Dan, I've built the entire technical platform. The repo originated in my org. I wrote the overwhelming majority of the code. I don't think anything below 60/40 reflects the contribution accurately. I'd rather we find a number we both feel good about."

*Your floor is 40%. Do not go below it.*

---

### Topic 3: IP License (3 min)

> "One thing I want to make sure is clean — the governance modules I built in the founder-mode repo, things like the delegation tokens, governance bus, CLP, compliance mapper — those are HUMMBL IP. foundermode-ai has a perpetual license to use them, but ownership stays with HUMMBL. Same way your iOS code is yours.
>
> If we ever went separate ways, you'd keep the app and everything integrated keeps working. I'd keep the governance stack. Clean separation. Fair?"

*This should be a quick yes.* Dan doesn't use the governance modules in the iOS app. If he pushes back here, it's a red flag — he'd be claiming ownership of code he didn't write for a purpose he doesn't use.

---

### Topic 4: Compensation (10 min)

*This is the hardest part. Take a breath before you start.*

> "Dan, I need to talk about compensation. Right now I get $430 a month. After I pay for Claude — which I just upgraded to the $200 tier because the work requires it — I take home $230. That's $1.44 an hour."

*Pause. Let that number land. Don't fill the silence.*

> "I'm not asking for market rate — a platform architect in Atlanta makes $180 to $220K. I know we're pre-revenue. But I need enough to cover my tools and basic expenses so I can stop job searching and focus on building this full-time.
>
> I'm thinking $3,000 a month. That's still an 80% discount on what I'd make as a consultant. It covers my tools, gives me some breathing room, and lets me commit fully to this instead of splitting my time with a job search."

*Wait for Dan's response.* Three scenarios:

**If Dan says yes to $3K**: Done. Move to vesting.

**If Dan says that's too much**:
> "What can you do? I need to be above $1,500 to make this sustainable. Could we do $2,000 now and step up to $3,000 when the app hits the App Store?"

**If Dan offers less than $1,500**:
> "I hear you, and I know money is tight pre-revenue. But at less than $1,500, I have to keep job searching, which means 15 to 20 hours a week on founder-mode instead of 40-plus. I don't want that and I know you don't either. Can we find a way to get to $2,000?"

**If Dan says he can't increase it at all**:
> "Dan, I need to be honest with you. $230 take-home isn't sustainable. People in my life are telling me I'm being taken advantage of, and the math backs them up. If we can't figure out compensation, I need to prioritize my job search and shift to part-time on founder-mode. That's not what I want, but I have to be realistic about my situation."

*Your floor is $1,000/month. Below that, reduce to part-time and focus on HUMMBL consulting + job search.*

---

### Topic 5: Vesting (3 min)

> "Last thing — vesting. I think standard 4-year vesting for both of us makes sense, backdated to when we started building. That way if either of us steps away, the other person isn't giving up equity to someone who stopped contributing. Standard startup stuff."

*This should be quick. Both sides benefit from vesting protection.*

---

### Closing (2 min)

> "Thanks for working through this with me, Dan. I feel good about where we landed. Here's what I'll do — I'll send you a summary email tonight with what we agreed on. Reply with any corrections. Then I'll draft a simple one-page agreement this week and we can both sign it.
>
> None of this changes how we work together. It just means we both know where we stand."

## Part 4: Negotiation Cheat Sheet

Keep this visible during the call.

| Topic | Your Ask | Your Floor | Walk-Away |
|-------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| foundermode-ai equity | 50/50 | 60/40 (Dan/Reuben) | Below 40% |
| HUMMBL ownership | 100% you | 100% you | Non-negotiable |
| HUMMBL advisory to Dan | 5% | 3% | 0% if Dan is difficult |
| IP license | HUMMBL owns, fm-ai licenses | Same | Non-negotiable |
| Monthly compensation | $3,000 | $1,500 | Below $1,000 = part-time |
| Vesting | 4yr, backdated | 4yr, current date | No vesting = no deal |

### Lines to remember

- *"$1.44 an hour."* — Your strongest single sentence. Say it and pause.
- *"The repo started in my org."* — Establishes provenance if equity gets contentious.
- *"I'm asking for an 80% discount on my market rate."* — Frames $3K as reasonable, not greedy.
- *"This protects both of us."* — Repeat this whenever the conversation feels tense.
- *"What feels right to you?"* — Use this to invite Dan into the number rather than reacting to yours.

### Things NOT to say

- Don't mention your girlfriend or her mother's opinion
- Don't say "I've been taken advantage of" — let the numbers speak
- Don't threaten to leave — state consequences factually ("I'd need to reduce hours")
- Don't apologize for asking — you're a co-founder negotiating fair terms
- Don't agree to anything in the moment if you're unsure — "Let me think about that and get back to you tomorrow" is always valid
